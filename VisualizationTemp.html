Latitude and Temperature (Fahrenheit)

There is a clear and obvious correlation between temperature and the latitude of the city. The temperature of cities is higher near the equator and lower in the higher latitudes. The large number of cities with a low temperature in the northern hemisphere is due to the amount of land in these areas and the larger number of cities in the northern hemisphere. The stablizing affect of the oceans on temperature is clear in this graph.

Latitude and Temperature (Fahrenheit) (Positional)

The visualization of temperature on the globe matches well with the graph shown above. Inland areas away from the equator are cooler than areas nearer the ocean. The Himalayan Mountains are also visible by the small concentration of cooler temperatures at about 30 degrees north latitude (about 75 degrees longitude).
